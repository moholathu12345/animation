Consultation Logs

A record of consultations aligned to my learning contract timeline, including feedback received and actions taken.

Consultation 1 — Portfolio setup + scope check

Week 1–2 (Planning / early setup)

Purpose: Clarify expectations for the module and ensure my plan was realistic (no over-proposal).

What I shared:

  • My intended final outcome (a short dance animation)
  • My planned weekly milestones and how I would document learning
  • My initial idea for the portfolio structure and pages

Feedback received:

  • Keep evidence minimal but strong (a few anchor pieces, not everything)
  • Make sure reflections explain decisions and changes, not just what I did
  • Keep the milestone timeline easy to scan for the assessor

Actions taken after:

  • Confirmed the project scope: one complete dance animation instead of multiple ambitious pieces
  • Mapped my Journey page to clear anchor weeks (Week 1–2, 4, 7, 9, 12)
  • Started building the portfolio pages (Home, Journey, Reflection, Consultations)

Outcome: Clear scope, clear documentation plan, and a portfolio structure that supports the module requirements.

Consultation 2 — Work-in-progress review + animation critique

Week 7–8 (Key poses / timing refinement phase)

Purpose: Show progress and get guidance on what to improve next.

What I shared:

  • Key poses and the sections I was struggling to make readable
  • Early timing adjustments and how I was comparing against reference
  • Short prototype clips to show progress and issues

Feedback received:

  • Pose clarity first, smoothness second (readability is the foundation)
  • Fix timing in sections instead of retiming the whole sequence repeatedly
  • Use reference to guide spacing and accents, not just the general vibe

Actions taken after:

  • Reworked key poses to be more readable before doing polish
  • Started locking timing one chunk at a time
  • Prepared the animation for beat sync by cleaning motion first

Outcome: My movement started to feel more intentional and less “floaty” after focusing on timing and pose clarity.

Logged

Consultation 3 — Music sync strategy + presentation planning

Week 9–10 (Beat matching + early camera/lighting planning)

Purpose: Confirm my approach for beat sync and avoid wasting time on presentation too early.

What I discussed:

  • How I was matching movement accents to beats and what felt “off”
  • Whether I should start camera and lighting before timing was fully locked
  • How to keep changes manageable without breaking the whole sequence

Guidance recorded:

  • Lock motion timing first, then add camera changes after
  • Keep camera simple and supportive so the dance stays readable
  • Do quick lighting tests for readability, not heavy effects

Actions taken after:

  • Retimed the sequence in sections instead of global adjustments
  • Planned basic camera framing once motion became stable
  • Tested lighting setups to keep the character clear against the background

Outcome: Beat sync improved without breaking the animation flow, and presentation planning stayed controlled.

Logged

Consultation 4 — Final readiness + portfolio narrative

Week 12–13 (Final rendering / effects + preparing to present learning)

Purpose: Ensure the final output and portfolio clearly show learning and meet assessment expectations.

What I discussed:

  • Final rendering plan (quality settings, consistency, and finishing touches)
  • What “effects” meant at my level (subtle polish vs overdoing it)
  • How to explain improvements during the interview

Guidance recorded:

  • Prioritise clean readability and consistency over flashy effects
  • Portfolio should read like growth: issue → change → result
  • Make sure Journey evidence links match the milestone timeline

Actions taken after:

  • Completed final render and ensured the piece felt cohesive
  • Aligned Journey weeks to the milestone timeline
  • Updated Reflection text to highlight decisions and improvement

Outcome: Final output completed and portfolio structured so the learning is clear and easy to assess.
